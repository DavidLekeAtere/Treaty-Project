Modern Day Context of Treaty 10

Study how treaty 10 affects the modern land and people involved in Treaty 10

The modern context of Treaty 10 is shaped by ongoing discussions about land rights, resource management, and the relationship between Indigenous peoples and the Canadian government. As society evolves, so too does the interpretation and implementation of treaties.



Creating Urban Reserves via Treaty Land Entitlements Under the original treaty, the federal government miscalculated population sizes, resulting in First Nations receiving much less reserve land than they were legally owed. For decades, leaders campaigned to correct these discrepancies.Through the Treaty Land Entitlement (TLE) Framework Agreement, Treaty 10 nations like English River fought for and won the right to buy land on the open market to expand their borders. Instead of accepting remote northern plots, they strategically bought land near major economic hubs. They established a highly successful urban reserve south of Saskatoon, using their treaty status to build commercial businesses, generate independent revenue, and create jobs for their members.


Here are some cases that helped usher improvements in first nations communities.

Case / Action First Nation Involved What They Stood Up For Modern Outcome
Oil Sands Legal Battle Buffalo River Dene Nation Opposed provincial bitumen exploration permits issued without consultation on traditional hunting and trapping grounds. Established a strict legal precedent forcing the government to consult before surface drilling can occur.
"Cows & Ploughs" Claim English River First Nation Held the federal government accountable for breaking a 1906 promise to provide livestock and farming supplies. Secured a major financial settlement from the federal government in 2024 for the historical breach.
Urban Reserve Creation English River First Nation Leveraged Treaty Land Entitlement (TLE) funds to claim land owed due to historical population miscalculations. Created a highly successful commercial urban reserve south of Saskatoon to generate independent revenue.
Treaty Ten Gatherings All Treaty 10 Member Nations United geographically isolated communities to protect oral histories and align political strategies against government overreach. Created a unified regional front that reinforces treaty rights through cultural preservation and collective political bargaining.
