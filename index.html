Welcome to the Treaty 10 Project

Exploring the history, lands, and communities involved in Treaty 10.

About Treaty 10

Treaty 10 was signed between 1906 and 1907 between the Canadian government and First Nations.

  • Signing Period: Treaty 10 was negotiated and signed between 1906 and 1907 involving the Cree and Chipewyan First Nations in northern Saskatchewan and parts of Alberta.
  • Treaty 10 was one of the last treaties signed in Saskatchewan and addressed Indigenous land rights.
  • The treaty was signed while the Canadian government was expanding control over western territories.

The Land & Communities

The communities involved in Treaty 10 primarily include the Cree and Chipewyan First Nations. These groups lived on the land covered by the treaty. The treaty included provisions for reserves, annuities, education, and the rights to hunt, fish, and trap.

Voices & Leaders

Chief William Apisis

Chief William Apisis was one of the Indigenous leaders involved in the Treaty 10 negotiations. He represented his community and worked to make sure their concerns about land, hunting, and their traditional way of life were heard during discussions with the Canadian government.

Commissioner J.A.J. McKenna

James Andrew Joseph McKenna was the Canadian government commissioner responsible for negotiating Treaty 10. His role was to travel to northern communities and explain the treaty terms to First Nations leaders before the agreement was signed in 1906 and 1907.

Cree and Chipewyan Leaders

Many Cree and Chipewyan leaders participated in the treaty discussions. These leaders spoke on behalf of their communities and wanted to ensure their people could continue to hunt, fish, and live on their traditional lands while also adapting to the changing political and economic conditions of the time.
